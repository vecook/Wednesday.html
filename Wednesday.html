DRESS Syndrome - When eosinophils get scary

Clinical presentation

A review of signs and sympoms

DRESS is a potentially-fatal, multi-system reaction characterized by exposure to a possible pharmacologic agent (drug), rash, eosinophilia and systemic symptoms

The rash is typically morbilliform and then coalesces into diffuse erythematous lesions. By definition, it covers more than 50% body surface area.

The most commonly implicated drugs are anti-epileptic medications, such as phenobarbital
